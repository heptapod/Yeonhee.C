Personal Introduction & Project Overview

About

Yeonhee Choi is a designer, researcher, and developer of this project which is deeply rooted in my firsthand experiences during COVID-19 in London, where I was suffering from intersectional vulnerability of (C)PTSD and ASD. It was painful as a person, but was an opportunity as a researcher to discover the critical gaps and insights to reimagine it through longitudinal research.

Skillset

Qualitative and Quantitative Research, Trauma-Informed Design, Participatory Design Research, Unity Development (C#), Physical Computing, Digital Fabrication, Tangible Interaction Design, Project Management longitudinal and cross-sectional study

Project Concept

Wish You Were Here is an accessible and affordable XR emergency aid for intersecting vulnerability of (C)PTSD and ASD, developed to tackle critical gaps in NHS and current XR digital therapeutics (DTx). Patients with (C)PTSD and ASD experience invisible emergency on daily basis due to trauma triggers and sensory overload. This can be painful affecting them functioning tasks. NHS service requires a long waiting time, not accessible for such difficult situation unless the patient is suicidal. This tool is for such invisible emergency situation within golden time and go about their life.

Core Innovation: This XR Experience provides immediate Contact Comfort of soft tactility with Positive Non-verbal, face expression that stimulate mirror neuron through physical tactile stimuli synced with AR.

Research Foundation

Harry Harlow's Contact Comfort Studies (1958): Foundational research demonstrating that physical comfort and contact are fundamental to emotional regulation and stress reduction, informing the tactile component of this design.
Temple Grandin's Deep Touch Pressure Research: Evidence that controlled pressure and tactile stimulation can significantly reduce anxiety and hypervigilance in neurodivergent individuals, directly influencing the heated water bottle interaction design.
Mirror Neuron Research: Mirror neuron studies show that observing positive facial expressions (such as smiling) activates corresponding neural pathways in the observer, triggering automatic emotional mirroring and promoting feelings of safety and connection. This non-verbal positive feedback mechanism is particularly effective for trauma survivors who may struggle with verbal communication during crisis states, as it bypasses cognitive processing and directly influences the emotional regulation system through embodied empathy responses.

Technical Implementation

Hardware Setup: Modified Google Cardboard viewer, heated water bottle (Fashy), and custom AR markers

Dual Marker System: Close-up view of hand and bottle markers used for AR tracking

Hand Interaction with Augmented Face Expressions

Two physical paper markers are attached to user's hand and the front center of the water bottle. The interaction system triggers 3 different 2D face expressions based on the relationship of the hand and bottle marker position.

1. Default

(If user's hand is not touching the bottle)

2. Small Smile

If user's hand is touching the bottle

3. Big Smile Ending

If cumulative hand interaction exceeds 20 seconds within 1 minute

Technical Features

  • Dual-marker AR tracking system (hand + bottle markers)
  • Real-time distance calculation (15cm threshold for touch simulation)
  • Adaptive timeout system (2-second buffer prevents accidental triggers)
  • Gamified yet Simple progression (20s/60s targets with Big Smile ending)

Reflection & Future Work

Meeting Project Aims

The project successfully addresses its core objective of creating an accessible, affordable mental health support tool for trauma survivors. By combining lived experience insights with evidence-based therapeutic approaches, "Wish You Were Here" demonstrates how XR technology can be democratized and made relevant to real-world mental health needs.

Challenges & Limitations

N=1 Research Limitations: As this solution is currently based on the developer's lived experience, broader validation is essential. The sensitive nature of trauma research requires careful ethical consideration and collaboration with clinical professionals for responsible scaling.

Solo Development Complexity: Managing the entire pipeline from research through technical implementation while navigating personal CPTSD symptoms presented significant challenges.

Future Development

Clinical Validation: Conduct interdisciplinary research in collaboration with other developers and medical experts and institutions such as NHS. I wish to further develop this initial prototype in participatory design method.

Community Impact: By demonstrating that lived experience can drive meaningful technological innovation, this project contributes to broader conversations about inclusive design and the importance of centering affected communities in solution development.
